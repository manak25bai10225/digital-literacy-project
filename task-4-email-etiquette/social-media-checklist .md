# Social Media Do's and Don'ts – College Student Guide
**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 – Part B**
Manak Agarwal | 25BAI10225 | CSE-AIML | 1st Year, VIT Bhopal University

---

## DO's

| # | Guideline |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Think before you post — once it's up, it's hard to take back. |
| 2 | Keep your profile set to private or friends-only on personal accounts. |
| 3 | Use a strong, unique password and enable two-factor authentication on every platform. |
| 4 | Give proper credit when you share someone else's work, photos, or ideas. |
| 5 | Report harmful or offensive content using the platform's built-in reporting tools. |
| 6 | Keep your college email separate from personal social media sign-ups. |

---

## DON'Ts

| # | Guideline |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Don't share your live location, home address, or daily routine publicly. |
| 2 | Don't post photos of exam papers, answer sheets, or internal college documents. |
| 3 | Don't engage with trolls — block, report, and move on. |
| 4 | Don't accept friend or follow requests from people you don't know in real life. |
| 5 | Don't post anything that could embarrass your college or a future employer. |
| 6 | Don't forward unverified news or messages without fact-checking first. |
